## Introduciton to physics and maths
In school, I never really understood maths and physics. Sure I was capable of manipulating equations to get the answer, but I never really understood it overall. This post is to help me understand the high level areas of maths and physics.

## Physics
Physics is the scientific study of matter, energy, and the forces of nature. Areas of physics include:
- Mechanics: Study of motion, forces and energy
- Thermodynamics: Heat, temperature, and energy transfers
- Electromagnetism: Electric and magnetic fields
- Optics: Behavior and properties of light
- Particle Physics and nuclear physics: Fundamental particles and their interactions
- Astrophysics: Planets, stars, galaxies, and the universe
- Quantum Mechanics

### Mechanics
- Kinematics: Motion, velocity, acceleration
- Newton's laws of motion
- Forces: Friction, gravity, tension, normal force
- Work, energy, and power
- Momentum and collisions
- Rotational motion and torque
- Simple harmonic motion

### Thermodynamics
- Temperature and heat
- Laws of thermodynamics
- Heat engines and efficiency
- Kinetic theory of gases
- Pressure in fluids
- Buoyancy and Archimedes' principle
- Fluid dynamics and Bernoulli's equation

### Electricity and Magnetism
- Electric charges and fields
- Electric potential and capacitance
- Current, resistance, and circuits
- Magnetic fields and forces
- Electromagnetic induction
- Maxwell's equations

### Waves and Optics
- Wave properties and behavior
- Sound waves
- Electromagnetic spectrum
- Reflection and refraction
- Interference and diffraction
- Lenses and mirrors

### Modern Physics
- Special relativity
- Quantum mechanics
- Atomic and nuclear physics
- Particle physics

### Astrophysics and Cosmology
- Stellar evolution
- Galaxies and the universe
- Big Bang theory
- Dark matter and dark energy

## Mathematics
Maths is the science of numbers, their operations, interrelation, and abstractions. Areas of maths include:
- Arithmetic: Basic operations with numbers
- Algebra: Use of symbols and letters to represent numbers and quantities
- Geometry: Study of shapes, sizes, and positions
- Trigonometry: Relationships between sides and angles of triangles
- Calculus: Study of continuous change
- Statistics and Probability: Analysis of data and likelihood of events

### Arithmetic and Number Theory
- Basic operations (addition, subtraction, multiplication, division)
- Fractions, decimals, and percentages
- Prime numbers and factorization
- Exponents and roots
- Modular arithmetic

### Algebra
- Variables and expressions
- Equations and inequalities
- Functions and graphs
- Polynomials
- Systems of equations

### Geometry
- Euclidean geometry
- Coordinate geometry
- Trigonometry
- Vectors

### Calculus
- Limits and continuity
- Derivatives
- Integrals
- Differential equations

### Linear Algebra
- Matrices and determinants
- Vector spaces
- Linear transformations
- Eigenvalues and eigenvectors

### Probability and Statistics
- Probability theory
- Descriptive statistics
- Inferential statistics
- Hypothesis testing
