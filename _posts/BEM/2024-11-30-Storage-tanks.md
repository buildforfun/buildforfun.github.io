## How do storage tanks work?

1. Cold water enters the tank from the bottom
2. Heating can occur via two methods:
   - Indirect heating: A coil inside the tank carries hot water from an external heat source (like a boiler or heat pump), which heats the water in the tank.
   - Direct heating: An electric immersion heater inside the tank heats the water directly.
3. Stratification:
   - hot water rises to the top due to stratification
   - layers water with hotest at the top
4. Tank insulated to minimise heat loss over time
5. Thermostat keeps temperature at a setpoint
6. Hot water drawn off from the top as needed.
7. As hot water is used, this is replaced with cold water at the bottom


## How Solar Thermal Works

### Overview of Solar Thermal Systems
Solar thermal systems harness sunlight to generate heat, which can then be used for various applications, primarily heating water for domestic or commercial use. Unlike photovoltaic systems that convert sunlight into electricity, solar thermal systems focus on capturing and utilizing thermal energy.

### Core Components of Solar Thermal Systems
1. **Solar Collectors**:
   - The primary component responsible for capturing solar energy. There are several types of solar collectors:
     - **Flat-Plate Collectors**: Typically used in residential applications, these consist of a flat, insulated box with a transparent cover and dark absorber plates.
     - **Evacuated Tube Collectors**: Comprising multiple glass tubes, these are more efficient in colder climates as they minimize heat loss.
   - The collectors contain a heat transfer fluid (often a mixture of water and antifreeze) that circulates through pipes embedded in the collector.

2. **Heat Exchanger**:
   - A crucial component that transfers heat from the heated fluid in the collector to the water stored in the tank.
   - Typically made of copper or another conductive material, the heat exchanger allows for efficient thermal transfer without mixing the fluids.

3. **Storage Tank**:
   - This tank stores the heated water for later use. It is insulated to minimize heat loss.
   - The tank usually has separate inlets and outlets for cold and hot water, allowing for efficient flow and usage.

4. **Control Unit**:
   - The control unit manages the operation of the system, including when to pump the heated fluid from the collector to the storage tank.
   - It typically includes sensors to monitor temperatures and ensure optimal performance.

5. **Pumps and Circulation System**:
   - Pumps are used to circulate the heat transfer fluid between the collectors and the storage tank.
   - A well-designed circulation system ensures efficient flow and minimal energy consumption.

### How Solar Thermal Systems Function
1. **Heat Absorption**:
   - Solar radiation hits the collector, heating the absorber plate. The heat transfer fluid circulating through the collector absorbs this heat.

2. **Heat Transfer**:
   - Once heated, the fluid is pumped through pipes to the heat exchanger located in the storage tank.
   - As the hot fluid flows through the heat exchanger, it transfers its thermal energy to the water in the tank, heating it up.

3. **Storage of Heated Water**:
   - The heated water is stored in the insulated tank until needed for use (e.g., for showers, washing dishes).
   - When hot water is drawn from the tank, cold water enters through a separate inlet, ensuring a continuous supply.

4. **Circulation Loop**:
   - After transferring its heat, the cooled fluid returns to the solar collector to be reheated, completing a closed-loop system.

### Applications of Solar Thermal Systems
- **Domestic Hot Water Heating**: Commonly used in residential settings to provide hot water for bathing, cooking, and cleaning.
- **Space Heating**: Can be integrated with underfloor heating or radiator systems to provide space heating during colder months.
- **Swimming Pool Heating**: Efficiently heats swimming pools by circulating pool water through solar collectors.
- **Industrial Applications**: Used in various industries for process heating or as part of larger systems like district heating.

### Benefits of Solar Thermal Systems
- **Energy Efficiency**: Significantly reduces reliance on fossil fuels by utilizing renewable energy from the sun.
- **Cost Savings**: Lower energy bills due to reduced consumption of conventional heating fuels.
- **Environmental Impact**: Contributes to lower greenhouse gas emissions by providing clean energy solutions.
- **Versatility**: Can be adapted for various applications and integrated with existing heating systems.

### Challenges and Considerations
- **Initial Costs**: The upfront investment for installation can be significant but is often offset by long-term savings.
- **Weather Dependency**: Performance can vary based on geographic location and weather conditions; backup systems may be needed during cloudy days or winter months.
- **Space Requirements**: Adequate roof space or land area is required for installation, particularly for larger systems.


## How a PV Diverter Works: From Basics

### Overview of PV Diverters
A photovoltaic (PV) diverter is a device that optimizes the use of excess electricity generated by solar panels. Instead of sending surplus energy back to the grid, a PV diverter redirects this energy to heat water, typically using an immersion heater. This allows homeowners to maximize the benefits of their solar power system and reduce reliance on conventional energy sources.

### Core Components of a PV Diverter
1. **Current Transformers (CT Clamps)**:
   - Two CT clamps are used to monitor electricity flow.
   - One clamp is attached to the solar PV inverter (which converts solar energy into usable electricity), and the other is connected to the electricity meter.
   - These sensors continuously measure the amount of electricity generated by the solar panels and how much is being consumed by the household.

2. **Control Unit**:
   - The control unit processes data from the CT clamps to determine when surplus electricity is available.
   - It manages the diversion of excess energy to the immersion heater or other appliances.

3. **Immersion Heater**:
   - The device that uses diverted electricity to heat water in a hot water tank.
   - It operates when there is surplus energy, ensuring that no generated power goes to waste.

### How PV Diverters Function
1. **Monitoring Energy Production and Consumption**:
   - The diverter continuously tracks how much energy the solar panels are producing and how much energy the household is using.
   - If the household's energy consumption is lower than what the solar panels generate, there will be excess energy available for diversion.

2. **Detecting Surplus Energy**:
   - When surplus energy is detected, the diverter activates and redirects this excess power away from the grid.
   - For example, if a solar system generates 4 kWh but the home only uses 2 kWh, the diverter will identify the 2 kWh surplus.

3. **Diverting Energy**:
   - The diverter sends this surplus electricity to power an immersion heater, which heats water in a storage tank.
   - This process allows homeowners to utilize free solar energy for heating water instead of relying on traditional heating methods.

4. **Real-Time Adjustments**:
   - The control unit makes real-time adjustments based on ongoing measurements of production and consumption.
   - If there is not enough surplus energy to meet heating demands, some diverters can switch modes and draw additional power from the grid as needed.

### Benefits of Using a PV Diverter
- **Maximized Solar Energy Use**: Homeowners can make full use of their solar power generation by utilizing excess energy for heating purposes.
- **Cost Savings**: By heating water with free solar energy, households can significantly reduce their reliance on gas or electric boilers, leading to lower utility bills.
- **Environmental Impact**: Utilizing renewable solar energy for heating reduces carbon emissions associated with traditional fossil fuel-based heating systems.
- **Versatility**: Many PV diverters can be integrated with existing hot water systems and can work alongside other technologies like battery storage or electric vehicle charging.

### Installation Considerations
- **Fitting Process**: Installing a PV diverter typically involves connecting it to the household's electrical panel and installing CT clamps around relevant cables. An electrician usually performs this installation.
- **Cost Implications**: The cost of installing a PV diverter can vary but generally adds around £800 to the total cost of a solar PV installation.

### Conclusion
A PV diverter enhances the efficiency of solar power systems by ensuring that surplus electricity is effectively utilized for heating water. By understanding how these devices work, homeowners can maximize their investment in solar technology while contributing to a more sustainable energy future. With benefits including cost savings and reduced environmental impact, PV diverters are an excellent addition for those looking to optimize their solar energy usage.
