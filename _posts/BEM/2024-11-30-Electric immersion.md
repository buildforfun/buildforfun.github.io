## How do electric immersion heaters work?

### Overview of Electric Immersion Heaters
Electric immersion heaters are devices used to heat water directly within a tank or cylinder. They operate on the principle of electric resistance heating, similar to how a kettle functions. An immersion heater consists of a metal coil or element that is immersed in the water it is meant to heat.

### Basic Components
- **Heating Element**: Typically made of metal (like copper or stainless steel), this coil or loop generates heat when an electric current passes through it.
- **Hot Water Cylinder**: The tank that holds water, where the immersion heater is installed.
- **Thermostat**: A device that regulates the water temperature by turning the heater on and off as needed.
- **Power Supply**: Immersion heaters are connected to the mains electricity, allowing them to be easily turned on or off.

### How Immersion Heaters Function
1. **Heating Process**:
   - When the immersion heater is switched on, electricity flows through the heating element, causing it to heat up.
   - The heat generated by the element is transferred directly to the surrounding water in the tank.
   - As the water heats up, it rises to the top of the tank due to convection, while cooler water enters from the bottom.

2. **Temperature Regulation**:
   - The thermostat monitors the water temperature and ensures it reaches a preset level (usually at least 50°C to kill bacteria).
   - Once the desired temperature is reached, the thermostat turns off the heater. If the temperature drops below this level, the heater activates again.

3. **Water Distribution**:
   - Hot water at the top of the tank is pushed out when cold water enters from below, maintaining pressure for taps and showers.
   - This process allows for continuous hot water supply as long as there is cold water entering the tank.

### Installation and Operation
- **Installation**: Immersion heaters can be installed in various configurations, including screw plug types that fit through threaded openings in tanks.
- **Control Mechanism**: Most immersion heaters come with an isolating switch for easy operation. Users can choose to heat water during off-peak electricity hours to save on energy costs.

### Benefits of Immersion Heaters
- **Versatility**: They can serve as primary or backup hot water systems, especially in homes without gas supply.
- **Efficiency**: When well-insulated, they can keep water warm for extended periods after being turned off.
- **Ease of Use**: Simple operation with just an on/off switch and thermostat control.

### Considerations
- **Cost of Operation**: While effective, immersion heaters tend to be more expensive to operate than gas heating systems due to electricity costs.
- **Heating Time**: It typically takes 1-2 hours for an immersion heater to heat a full tank of water, depending on its capacity and initial temperature.
