## Numerical Evidence for Global Warming

The evidence for global warming is supported by various numerical data that illustrate the changes in climate patterns, temperature increases, and other significant environmental shifts. Below are key statistics that highlight the extent of global warming and its implications.

### 1. Global Temperature Increases
- **Average Surface Temperature Rise**: Since 1880, Earth's average surface temperature has risen by approximately **1.8°F (1.0°C)**.
- **Recent Decadal Trends**: Each of the last four decades has been warmer than any other decade since 1850, with land temperatures rising even faster, reaching around **1.7°C** above pre-industrial levels in recent years.

### 2. Atmospheric Carbon Dioxide Levels
- **Pre-Industrial CO2 Levels**: The concentration of carbon dioxide (CO2) in the atmosphere was about **280 parts per million (ppm)** before the industrial era.
- **Current CO2 Levels**: As of 2019, CO2 levels have increased to approximately **410 ppm**, representing a rise of over **40%** since pre-industrial times.

### 3. Sea Level Rise
- **Historical Sea Level Increase**: Between 1901 and 2018, the average global sea level rose by about **15–25 cm (6–10 inches)**, with a rate of increase of approximately **2.3 mm (0.091 inches) per year since the 1970s**.
- **Recent Acceleration**: The rate of sea level rise has accelerated to about **4.62 mm (0.182 inches) per year** for the decade from 2013 to 2022.

### 4. Melting Ice and Glacial Retreat
- **Greenland Ice Sheet Loss**: The Greenland ice sheet is melting at an accelerating rate, contributing significantly to sea level rise.
- **Glacier Retreat**: Mountain glaciers worldwide are retreating, with observable impacts on local ecosystems and water supplies.

### 5. Human Influence on Climate
- **Scientific Consensus**: According to the Intergovernmental Panel on Climate Change (IPCC), human activities are responsible for nearly **100% of the warming observed since 1950**, with fossil fuel emissions being the primary contributor.
- **Temperature Attribution**: Models indicate that human-induced factors account for approximately **104% of the total warming** observed since the start of the modern period in 1950.

## Conclusion
The numerical evidence presented underscores a clear trend of global warming driven primarily by human activities. Rising temperatures, increasing atmospheric CO2 levels, accelerating sea level rise, and extensive glacial melt all point to significant changes in Earth's climate system that require urgent attention and action.
