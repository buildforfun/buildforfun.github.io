# how to master life

You are born into this world in a random location, after beating about 400 trillion other possible people.

![Untitled](/assets/Untitled.png)

# childhood

This is the stage where your parents decisions are ultimately what you do. You basially have no say - you make lots of mistakes all the time. You end up copying what others are doing most the time to get along. Usually there is a constant search for approval and validation in this stage.

![Untitled](/assets/Untitled%201.png)

# young adult life

You have now reached an age where you need to start deciding things for yourself. You have to decide things that normally others would have done for you.

If you don’t know what to do - don’t worry. Most people don’t find what they want to do in life until much later in life and some never do. To maximise your chances of finding what you want to do - **build useful skills and knowledge. The more you know the more likely you are able to perceive what is your calling.** 

Remember: you are in a time where you have lots of energy and time. Use it wisely - when you are old you have little energy and time.

![Untitled](/assets/Untitled%202.png)

So how do you assign your life as well as possible?

# learn the big ideas in the big disciplines

What I noted since the really big ideas carry 95% of the freight, it wasn’t at all hard for me to pick up all the big ideas from all the big disciplines and make them a standard part of my mental routines. Once you have the ideas, of course, they are no good if you don’t practice — if you don’t practice you lose it.

So I went through life constantly practicing this model of the multidisciplinary approach. Well, I can’t tell you what that’s done for me. It’s made life more fun, it’s made me more constructive, it’s made me more helpful to others, it’s made me enormously rich, you name it, that attitude really helps.

Now there are dangers there, because it works so well, that if you do it, you will frequently find you are sitting in the presence of some other expert, maybe even an expert that’s superior to you, supervising you. And you will know more than he does about his own specialty, a lot more. You will see the correct answer when he’s missed it.

It doesn’t help you just to know them enough just so you can give them back on an exam and get an A. You have to learn these things in such a way that they’re in a [**mental latticework](https://www.notion.so/Mental-models-9a0a24f57d3448e3ae2c98c053cce86b?pvs=21)** in your head and you automatically use them for the rest of your life.

![Untitled](/assets/Untitled%203.png)

# pick some valuable skills then get good at them

Some skills are more valuable than others. Good ones can open up whole paths.

![Untitled](/assets/Untitled%204.png)

The inverse of that is bad choices can lead to poor bad pathways.

![Untitled](/assets/Untitled%205.png)

Combinations of skills are the most effective. It’s very hard to max out one skill to be the best – in fact, that’s often impossible. But it’s much easier to get pretty decent at lots of related skills that amount to something bigger.

Individual skills are common. Combinations are rarer. If you want to raise your value, take a step back from your strengths, and consider building a broader combination of them.

# feeling unable to do what you want

You find that sometimes when you choose to do something – say “go for a run” – your body wil ignore you completely. This is not a bug. Everybody has a state: 

![Untitled](/assets/Untitled%206.png)

Your energy level is especially important.  Energy is replenished slightly by eating, and completely by a good night’s sleep. When your energy is low, you are only able to do things you really want to or is really easy to do - like watch videos all day.

Every decision you have to make costs energy, and decisions where you have to suppress an appealing option for a less appealing one (e.g. exercise instead of watch TV) require a *lot* of energy.

# source of unlimited energy - dopamine (life force)

dopamine is the chemical for motivation. No one else gives you dopamine. You generate your own dopamine. You have to set lots of mini challenges, when you achieve those mini challenges and then ponder on the achievement, your body will generate more dopamine which will be converted into epinephrine(adrenaline) that can be used to achieve the next challenge.

![Untitled](/assets/Untitled%207.png)

Learn to spike dopamine from the **challenge/effort/pain** itself - make effort the reward. The philosophy behind this is **Growth Mindset.**

Growth mindset says intelligence can be developed. This leads to embracing challenges, persist in the face of setbacks, see effort as  a path to mastery, learn from critism and find lessons and inspiration in success of others.

![Untitled](/assets/Untitled%208.png)

If we invert the growth mindset, we get the fixed mindset. They avoid challenges, give up easily, see effort as fruitless, ignore useful negative feedback, feel threatened by the success of others and tend to plateau early achieve less than potential. 

![Untitled](/assets/Untitled%209.png)

# life impact

As you learn more skills you will form an intense interest in particular topics. Capitalise on those. Spend an unfathamable/irrational amount of time on those. If doubt sets in on if that is the thing you want to focus on, stick with it and come back to that feeling if it persists for a long time. As you iterate and iterate, you’ll get better and better at what you want to do.

![Untitled](/assets/Untitled%2010.png)

Life is substantiated by whatever impact you can make or whatever contribution you can make. What conttribution can you make?  

Freetime is the enemy of progress. 

# find what makes you happy and literally do it every single day
