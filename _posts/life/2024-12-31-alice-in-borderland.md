Alice in Borderland features a series of deadly games that test the characters' physical, mental, and emotional limits. Each game is categorized by the suits of a deck of cards, with specific themes and rules that dictate how players must compete. Here’s an overview of the various games presented throughout the series.

## Card Suits and Game Types

The games are divided into four suits, each representing different types of challenges:

- **Clubs**: Focus on teamwork and cooperation.
- **Diamonds**: Require intelligence and strategic thinking.
- **Hearts**: Involve psychological manipulation and emotional stakes.
- **Spades**: Center on physical strength and survival.

# Alice in Borderland Games and Solutions

In *Alice in Borderland*, each game presents unique challenges and potential solutions. Below is a breakdown of notable games along with their solutions.

## Game Solutions

### 1. Seven of Hearts (Hide and Seek)
- **Game Overview**: One player is designated as the 'wolf', while others are 'sheep'. The objective is to avoid being caught.
- **Solution**: A common theory suggests that the sheep can cut the wire on the wolf's collar, effectively disconnecting it from the explosive device. By doing this, one sheep can take on the role of the wolf, allowing the remaining sheep to do the same sequentially until only one wolf remains, who can then be safely disconnected from danger.

### 2. Four of Diamonds
- **Game Overview**: Players must determine which of three switches activates a light bulb in a room before water rises and electrocutes them.
- **Solution**: The winning strategy involves one player entering the room to test switches while others remain outside. They can first turn on two switches (A and B), then check if the light is on when they open the door. If it's off, switch C is correct; if it's on, they can deduce which switch activated it by checking the heat of the bulb after closing the door again.

### 3. Dead or Alive (Three of Clubs)
- **Game Overview**: Players must choose between two doors, one labeled 'Alive' and one 'Die'.
- **Solution**: While no definitive solution is provided in the series, players could theoretically test doors without entering by throwing objects or using other means to determine if a room is rigged before making a choice.

### 4. Checkmate (Queen of Spades)
- **Game Overview**: A physical game where players must tag each other while avoiding capture by the opposing team.
- **Solution**: The key to winning involves teamwork and strategy, convincing others to fight for their side rather than surrendering to the Queen's team. Forming alliances and coordinating attacks are crucial.

### 5. Osmosis (King of Clubs)
- **Game Overview**: A team-based game where players must manage points while competing against citizens.
- **Solution**: A viable strategy is to allocate most points to one player while others protect their base. This allows for more effective battles, as having a strong point holder increases chances of winning against opponents.

### 6. Witch Hunt (Ten of Hearts)
- **Game Overview**: Players must identify a 'witch' among them within a time limit.
- **Solution**: The solution lies in careful observation and deduction based on players' behaviors and interactions, fostering paranoia that can lead to mistakes among players.

### 7. Beauty Contest (King of Diamonds)
- **Game Overview**: Players choose numbers between 0 and 100; the closest average wins.
- **Solution**: The game requires anticipating opponents' choices and adjusting strategies based on previous rounds, as players tend to lower their numbers over time to avoid elimination.

These solutions reflect both strategic thinking and teamwork, essential for survival in the brutal world of *Alice in Borderland*. Each game tests not only physical abilities but also mental acuity and social dynamics among players.
