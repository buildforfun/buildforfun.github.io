## Design like an Engineer

If a customer came to you and asked you to “Make me something that can bring my coffee to me when I’m in my chair.” As an Engineer you may be faced with request like this.

## **Part 1: Figuring out what the customer wants**

Before you can solve a problem, you need to clearly define what it is. This involves breaking it down into smaller, more manageable parts and identifying the root cause of the issue.

Firstly, the request is too vague. This means there are many possible solutions and it is likely the customer already has a preference in mind. This is where the customer and engineer will have to work together to clearly define the problem and what the customer is looking for. For example, a more cleary defined request would be:

*“Design and manufacture a vehicle in 6 months a ground based vehicle that is capable of travelling maximum speed of 5km/h, dimensions limited to half a metre cube and weigh less than 5kg that can lift and carry a payload up to 0.5kg.”*

These constraints are then used to create a design specification. These are documents which have specific requirements of the design for the customer.

| Constraints | Numerical limitations |
| --- | --- |
| Max Speed | <5km/h |
| Max Payload weight | up to 0.5kg |
| Vehicle weight | <5kg |
| Max dimensions | 0..5m^3 |
| Remote control | Radio |
| Battery | Must be certified |
| Manufacturing cost per unit | <£250 |

*A short example of a design specification*

## **Part 2: Generating Ideas**

The problem is then broken into smaller areas. In this example, it might be electronics, structure ..etc

Ideas are generated for solutions using the design specification within each of the areas. Ideas are best generated when working in teams. Individuals are expected to conduct research and bring various ideas to discuss and collaborate on.

Sketches are often made of how the idea will be practically made. Then each part is dimensioned using engineering drawing.

The ideas are then ranked according according to what engineers deem to be most crucial. For example if dimensions was the most important part it would be given 10pts while speed was not as prevalent then it would be given 5pts. This would then weight the overall score that can be used to compare different designs.

## **Part 3: Test the best solution**

Once the best idea is selected, a detailed plan is created and executed. Product testing helps look for further improvements within its constraints and to see whether it in practice it the soluation fails.

## **Quick summary:**

- Ensure you have a clear specification
- Work in teams to generate ideas
- Test your idea thoroughly
