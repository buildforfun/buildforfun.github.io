## Physics
Physics is the scientific study of matter, energy, and the forces of nature. Areas of physics include:
- Mechanics: Study of motion, forces and energy
- Thermodynamics: Heat, temperature, and energy transfers
- Electromagnetism: Electric and magnetic fields
- Optics: Behavior and properties of light
- Particle Physics and nuclear physics: Fundamental particles and their interactions
- Astrophysics: Planets, stars, galaxies, and the universe
- Quantum Mechanics

## Mechanics
Mechanics is the branch of physics dealing with motion and forces.

### Kinematics
Kinematics describes motion without considering its causes.

- **Displacement (Δx)**: The change in position of an object. Δx = xf - xi
- **Velocity (v)**: The rate of change of displacement. v = Δx / Δt
- **Acceleration (a)**: The rate of change of velocity. a = Δv / Δt

**Equations of Motion** (for constant acceleration):
1. v = u + at
2. s = ut + ½at²
3. v² = u² + 2as

Where:
- v: final velocity
- u: initial velocity
- a: acceleration
- t: time
- s: displacement

These equations allow us to solve problems involving motion when acceleration is constant.

### Newton's Laws of Motion
Newton's laws form the foundation of classical mechanics.

1. **First Law**: An object remains at rest or in uniform motion unless acted upon by a net external force.
   - This law introduces the concept of inertia.

2. **Second Law**: F = ma
   - The acceleration of an object is directly proportional to the net force acting on it and inversely proportional to its mass.

3. **Third Law**: For every action, there is an equal and opposite reaction.
   - This law describes the nature of forces as interactions between objects.

### Forces
Forces are pushes or pulls acting on an object.

- **Friction**: f = μN 
  - μ is the coefficient of friction, N is the normal force
  - Friction opposes motion between surfaces in contact

- **Gravity**: F = GMm/r²
  - G is the gravitational constant, M and m are masses, r is the distance between centers
  - This is Newton's law of universal gravitation

- **Tension**: Force exerted by a string, rope, or cable on an object

- **Normal force**: Force exerted by a surface on an object in contact with it, perpendicular to the surface

### Work, Energy, and Power
These concepts are closely related and fundamental to understanding energy transfers.

- **Work**: W = F · d · cos(θ)
  - Work is done when a force moves an object
  - θ is the angle between the force and displacement vectors

- **Kinetic Energy**: KE = ½mv²
  - Energy of motion

- **Potential Energy**:
  - Gravitational: PE = mgh (near Earth's surface)
  - Elastic: PE = ½kx² (for springs, where k is spring constant)

- **Conservation of Energy**: In a closed system, total energy remains constant
  - ΔKE + ΔPE = 0

- **Power**: P = W/t = F · v
  - Rate of doing work or transferring energy

### Momentum and Collisions
Momentum is a measure of the motion of an object, considering both mass and velocity.

- **Linear Momentum**: p = mv

- **Conservation of Momentum**: Total momentum before collision = Total momentum after collision
  - p1i + p2i = p1f + p2f (for a two-object system)

- **Impulse**: J = F · Δt = Δp
  - Change in momentum equals the impulse applied

### Rotational Motion
Rotational motion involves objects rotating about an axis.

- **Angular Velocity (ω)**: ω = dθ/dt
  - Rate of change of angular position

- **Angular Acceleration (α)**: α = dω/dt
  - Rate of change of angular velocity

- **Torque (τ)**: τ = r × F = rF sin(θ)
  - Rotational equivalent of force

- **Moment of Inertia (I)**: I = Σmr²
  - Rotational equivalent of mass

- **Rotational Kinetic Energy**: KE = ½Iω²

### Simple Harmonic Motion
Simple harmonic motion is a type of periodic motion where the restoring force is proportional to displacement.

- **Period (T)**: T = 2π√(m/k) (for a spring-mass system)
  - Time for one complete oscillation

- **Frequency (f)**: f = 1/T
  - Number of oscillations per unit time

- **Angular Frequency (ω)**: ω = 2πf

## Thermodynamics

Thermodynamics deals with heat, temperature, and their relation to energy and work.

### Temperature and Heat
Temperature is a measure of the average kinetic energy of particles in a substance, while heat is the transfer of thermal energy.

- **Temperature conversion**:
  - °F = (9/5 × °C) + 32
  - K = °C + 273.15

- **Heat Capacity**: Q = mcΔT
  - Q: heat energy, m: mass, c: specific heat capacity, ΔT: change in temperature

### Laws of Thermodynamics
These laws describe how thermal energy is converted and transferred.

1. **Zeroth Law**: If A is in thermal equilibrium with B, and B with C, then A is in thermal equilibrium with C.
   - This law defines thermal equilibrium.

2. **First Law**: ΔU = Q - W
   - Energy is conserved; it can be converted from one form to another but not created or destroyed.
   - ΔU: change in internal energy, Q: heat added to system, W: work done by system

3. **Second Law**: The entropy of an isolated system always increases.
   - This law introduces the concept of entropy and the irreversibility of natural processes.

4. **Third Law**: As temperature approaches absolute zero, the entropy of a perfect crystal approaches zero.
   - This law provides an absolute reference point for entropy.

### Heat Engines and Efficiency
Heat engines convert thermal energy to mechanical work.

- **Efficiency**: η = W/Qh = 1 - Qc/Qh = 1 - Tc/Th
  - η: efficiency, W: work output, Qh: heat input, Qc: heat rejected, Th: hot reservoir temperature, Tc: cold reservoir temperature

### Kinetic Theory of Gases
This theory explains macroscopic properties of gases in terms of their microscopic behavior.

- **Ideal Gas Law**: PV = nRT
  - P: pressure, V: volume, n: number of moles, R: gas constant, T: temperature

- **Average Kinetic Energy**: KE = (3/2)kT
  - k: Boltzmann constant

## Electricity and Magnetism

Electromagnetism unifies electric and magnetic phenomena.

### Electric Charges and Fields
Electric charges create electric fields, which exert forces on other charges.

- **Coulomb's Law**: F = k(q1q2/r²)
  - F: force, k: Coulomb's constant, q1 and q2: charges, r: distance between charges

- **Electric Field**: E = F/q = kQ/r²
  - E: electric field strength, Q: source charge

### Electric Potential and Capacitance
Electric potential is the potential energy per unit charge in an electric field.

- **Electric Potential**: V = W/q = kQ/r
  - V: electric potential, W: work done to move charge q from infinity to point r

- **Capacitance**: C = Q/V
  - C: capacitance, Q: charge stored, V: potential difference

- **Energy Stored in Capacitors**: U = ½CV²

### Current, Resistance, and Circuits
Electric current is the flow of electric charge.

- **Ohm's Law**: V = IR
  - V: voltage, I: current, R: resistance

- **Resistors in Series**: Req = R1 + R2 + R3 + ...

- **Resistors in Parallel**: 1/Req = 1/R1 + 1/R2 + 1/R3 + ...

### Magnetic Fields and Forces
Moving charges create magnetic fields, which exert forces on other moving charges.

- **Magnetic Force on Moving Charges**: F = qv × B
  - F: force, q: charge, v: velocity, B: magnetic field

- **Biot-Savart Law**: dB = (μ0/4π) · (Idl × r̂/r²)
  - Describes the magnetic field created by a current-carrying wire

### Electromagnetic Induction
Changing magnetic fields induce electric fields and vice versa.

- **Faraday's Law**: ε = -dΦB/dt
  - ε: induced emf, ΦB: magnetic flux

- **Lenz's Law**: The direction of induced current opposes the change in magnetic flux that caused it.

### Maxwell's Equations
These equations summarize the fundamental laws of electromagnetism:

1. Gauss's Law for Electricity: ∮E·dA = Q/ε0
2. Gauss's Law for Magnetism: ∮B·dA = 0
3. Faraday's Law: ∮E·dl = -dΦB/dt
4. Ampère-Maxwell Law: ∮B·dl = μ0I + μ0ε0dΦE/dt

## Waves and Optics

This branch deals with the behavior of waves, including light.

### Wave Properties and Behavior
- **Wave Equation**: v = fλ
  - v: wave speed, f: frequency, λ: wavelength

### Sound Waves
Sound waves are longitudinal waves that propagate through a medium.

- **Speed of Sound**: v = √(γP/ρ)
  - γ: adiabatic index, P: pressure, ρ: density

### Electromagnetic Spectrum
The EM spectrum includes all types of electromagnetic radiation.

- **Energy of a Photon**: E = hf = hc/λ
  - h: Planck's constant, c: speed of light

### Reflection and Refraction
These phenomena occur when waves encounter boundaries between media.

- **Law of Reflection**: θi = θr
  - Angle of incidence equals angle of reflection

- **Snell's Law**: n1sin(θ1) = n2sin(θ2)
  - Describes refraction of light between media with different refractive indices

### Interference and Diffraction
These phenomena result from the wave nature of light.

- **Double-Slit Interference**: dsin(θ) = mλ
  - d: slit separation, θ: angle to interference maximum, m: order of maximum

- **Single-Slit Diffraction**: asin(θ) = mλ
  - a: slit width

### Lenses and Mirrors
Lenses and mirrors manipulate light paths to form images.

- **Thin Lens Equation**: 1/f = 1/do + 1/di
  - f: focal length, do: object distance, di: image distance

- **Mirror Equation**: 1/f = 1/do + 1/di

- **Magnification**: M = -di/do

## Modern Physics

Modern physics encompasses theories developed in the 20th century and beyond.

### Special Relativity
Einstein's theory dealing with motion at high speeds.

- **Time Dilation**: t = t0 / √(1 - v²/c²)
- **Length Contraction**: L = L0√(1 - v²/c²)
- **Mass-Energy Equivalence**: E = mc²

### Quantum Mechanics
Describes behavior at the atomic and subatomic level.

- **de Broglie Wavelength**: λ = h/p
- **Heisenberg Uncertainty Principle**: ΔxΔp ≥ ħ/2

### Atomic and Nuclear Physics
Deals with the structure and behavior of atoms and nuclei.

- **Binding Energy**: ΔE = Δmc²
- **Radioactive Decay**: N = N0e^(-λt)

### Particle Physics
Studies fundamental particles and their interactions.

- Standard Model: Describes fundamental particles and forces

## Astrophysics and Cosmology

This field studies celestial objects and the universe as a whole.

### Stellar Evolution
- **Stefan-Boltzmann Law**: L = 4πR²σT⁴
  - L: luminosity, R: radius, σ: Stefan-Boltzmann constant, T: surface temperature

### Galaxies and the Universe
- **Hubble's Law**: v = H0d
  - v: recessional velocity, H0: Hubble constant, d: distance

### Big Bang Theory
Explains the origin and evolution of the universe.

- Cosmic Microwave Background Radiation: T ≈ 2.7 K

### Dark Matter and Dark Energy
Mysterious components that make up most of the universe's mass-energy.

- **Critical Density**: ρc = 3H0²/(8πG)
  - G: gravitational constant
