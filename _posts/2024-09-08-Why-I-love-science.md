## Why I love science

Growing up, my parents told me that we were hindu, I as most kids do trusted their parents and this is what I believed in. I must say at the time I did experience a feeling of being part of a group which I liked a lot. Going to temples and see the artwork and rituals did fascinate me. I found the stories my mum would tell me about the hindu gods further fascinating. However, one day, my mum told me this story about how this diety got an elephant head. I won't go into the whole story, but when she said the god's dad found an elephant, cut off the elepahnt head and stuck it on god's neck, I called bullshit. The story made no sense, and when I asked my mum any more questions she just said it's the truth and not to question it. Since then, I have not been religious. Many years later, I watched this clip of Rick Gervais where he said something along the lines of if you take all the science books and burnt them, in a thousand years they would come back the same, wheras if you did the same to relgious books, they would come back different. This really resonated with me and told me not not to trust in relgions.

On the other hand, going to school and being taught science, I started slowly understanding that you can ask science the why questions and it could answer them. Before we go on, let's define what Science is. Science is the understanding of the universe based on empirical evidence. The empirical evidence sets science apart from other belief systems. 

There are cases where science doesn't have the evidence yet, and this is because more research is needed. For example, we don't know what happened before the big bang, but science doesn't try to reason it away. Instead there are possible theories, but nothing is taught as the facts.

Science also gives us a framework to acquire new knowledge - the Scientific Method. The stages of the scientific method:
1. Observation: An observation is made 
2. Questioning of observation: The observation is questioned to see if there any problems
3. Further research: The area is researched to find out if there is already any existing solutions.
4. Hypothesis: A hypothesis is formed
5. Predictions: A prediction is made, that can be tested.
6. Experiment: An experiment is carried out and data is collated
7. Data analysis: The data is analysed to identify any patterns.
8. Conclusion: Determine if hypothesis is proven based on data.
9. Peer review: The findings are submitted for other scientists to critically evaluate the findings.

Experiments need to be design in a way that they are reproducible (the results are consistent when the experiment is repeated) and accurate (the results are close to the true value). This open transparency allows others to test the claim and reject it, if found to be incorrect. I love that science allows others to test claims that are made before it is accepted.

Finally, I love that science gives you the tools to think clearly. It forces you constantly ask why and how to all problems. It makes you visualise and understand complicated systems and to make judgements. Other subjects often teach you to memorise vast contents but don't test your ability to apply as much as science. Most the knowledge is now also easily searchable digitally, so the ability to memorise a lot of content isn't as important as it once was. These are some of the reasons i love science, there are many more, but I think that is enough for this post. Remember keep on learning!
