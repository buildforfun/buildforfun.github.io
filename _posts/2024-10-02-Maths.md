## Mathematics
Maths is the science of numbers, their operations, interrelation, and abstractions. Areas of maths include:
- Arithmetic: Basic operations with numbers
- Algebra: Use of symbols and letters to represent numbers and quantities
- Geometry: Study of shapes, sizes, and positions
- Trigonometry: Relationships between sides and angles of triangles
- Calculus: Study of continuous change
- Statistics and Probability: Analysis of data and likelihood of events


## What is Calculus?

Calculus is a way to understand how things change and how to find areas. It has two main parts:

1. **Differential Calculus**: This helps us find out how fast something is changing.
2. **Integral Calculus**: This helps us find the total amount of something, like area under a curve.

## 1. Basic Concepts

### 1.1 Functions

- A **function** is like a machine that takes an input and gives an output.
- Example: If you have a function f(x) = x + 2, and you put in 3 (f(3)), it gives you 5 (3 + 2).

### 1.2 Graphs of Functions

- Graphs show how functions behave.
- The **x-axis** is for inputs, and the **y-axis** is for outputs.
- Each point on the graph represents a pair (input, output).

## 2. Limits

Limits help us understand what happens to a function as we get close to a certain point.

### 2.1 Understanding Limits

- When we say "the limit as x approaches 3 of f(x) equals 5," it means that as x gets closer to 3, f(x) gets closer to 5.

### 2.2 Evaluating Limits

You can find limits by:
- **Direct substitution**: Just plug in the number.
- **Factoring**: Simplify the expression if needed.

## 3. Derivatives

Derivatives tell us how quickly something is changing at any point.

### 3.1 Definition of a Derivative

- The derivative of a function at a point gives the slope of the line tangent to the graph at that point.

### 3.2 How to Think About Derivatives

- If you think of a car's position over time, the derivative tells you its speed at any moment.

### 3.3 Common Rules for Finding Derivatives

1. **Power Rule**: For f(x) = x², the derivative f'(x) = 2x.
2. **Sum Rule**: If you have two functions added together, just take their derivatives separately.

## 4. Integrals

Integrals are used to find areas under curves or total quantities.

### 4.1 Indefinite Integrals

An indefinite integral gives you a function whose derivative is the original function:
- Example: ∫ x dx = (1/2)x² + C (where C is a constant).

### 4.2 Definite Integrals

A definite integral calculates the area under the curve from one point to another:
- Example: ∫[1,3] (x²) dx gives the area under the curve from x=1 to x=3.

## Applications of Calculus

Calculus has many real-world uses:

1. **Physics**: Understanding motion and forces.
2. **Economics**: Finding maximum profit or minimum cost.
3. **Biology**: Modeling population growth or decay.

## Summary

Calculus helps us understand change and accumulation in simple terms:
- **Derivatives** tell us how fast things change.
- **Integrals** help us find total amounts or areas.

By learning these basics, you'll be equipped to explore more complex ideas in calculus as you progress!
