#### Learning muay thai
The foundation of Muay Thai starts with proper stance and footwork. A strong stance provides balance and power, while effective footwork allows for fluid movement and positioning.

A Roundhouse kick (ai gen text)
----
- **Foot Position**: Keep your rear foot on the ball of your foot to maintain mobility and balance. This allows for easier pivoting and hip rotation.

- **Step Forward**: Take a slight step at a 45-degree angle with your lead leg towards the target. This helps align your body with the center line of your target.
- **Arm Movement**: As you step, swing your rear arm back while bringing your lead hand down. This motion helps pull your hip forward and prepares for the kick.

- **Lift Your Knee**: Drive your knee upward towards the target first, which helps generate power from your hips rather than just swinging your leg.
- **Pivot Your Foot**: Rotate your post foot (the one not kicking) outward as you kick. This pivot opens up your hips and maximizes power. Think of it like you are humping the air.

- **Follow Through**: Extend your leg fully while keeping your foot flexed (toes pointed) for impact. Aim to kick through the target rather than just making contact.

- **Return to Stance**: After the kick, quickly bring your kicking leg back to its original position and return to a defensive stance. Ensure that you land balanced to avoid being vulnerable after the strike.

Tips:
- **Stay Relaxed**: Keep your body loose; tension can hinder movement and reduce power.
- **Chamber Your Kick**: Always chamber (bend) your kicking leg before extending it to maintain speed and control.
- **Watch Your Balance**: Ensure that when you land after a kick, you do so without crossing your feet or losing balance.

Switch kick (ai gen text)
----
A Muay Thai switch kick is a powerful technique that involves quickly changing your stance before delivering a kick. Here's how to execute it effectively:

### Setup and Stance

- Start in your regular Muay Thai stance with your weight slightly on your back foot.
- Keep your front foot light on the ground to facilitate a smooth switch.

### Executing the Switch

- Perform a quick skip to switch your stance, bringing your rear foot forward and your front foot back.
- Keep your feet closer together than in your normal stance, staying on the balls of your feet.
- As you switch, swing your rear arm down and your lead arm up across your body.

### Delivering the Kick

- Immediately after switching, explode off your new rear foot (formerly your front foot).
- Rotate on the ball of your new lead foot as you kick.
- Turn your hip into the kick, thrusting it forward for power.
- Swing your kicking-side arm downward while keeping your other arm up for balance and power.

### Key Points to Remember

- Keep the switch narrow and stay on your toes to spring into the kick quickly.
- Aim to have your non-kicking foot pointing towards "1 or 2 o'clock" to facilitate easier pivoting.
- Keep your eyes on the target and your chin down.
- Aim to impact with your lower shin.
- Follow through with the kick and immediately return to your original stance.

Tips:
- Practice switching often as feints to keep your opponent guessing.
- Stay level during the switch to avoid telegraphing your intent.
- Use the switch kick to set up other techniques, like a double switch followed by punches.

Some random tips:
----
  - Use punches first to distract the opponent. Then kick when they can't see it coming
  - Understand your range/bubble - will my hand/foot reach target?
  - Feint a lot - throws opponent off
  - See the opening for a kick - where are they exposed?
  - Whip hands faster to get more power on kicks
