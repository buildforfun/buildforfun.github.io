# The History of Britain from 1900 to the Modern Period

## Early 20th Century (1900-1945)

### 1901-1910: The Edwardian Era
- **1901**: Death of Queen Victoria; King Edward VII ascends the throne.
- **1903**: Formation of the Women’s Social and Political Union to campaign for women’s suffrage.
- **1904**: Britain signs the Entente Cordiale with France, marking a significant diplomatic agreement.
- **1906**: Launch of the first Dreadnought battleship, escalating the naval arms race with Germany.

### 1910-1918: World War I
- **1910**: King George V begins his reign.
- **1914**: Outbreak of World War I; Britain enters the war against Germany.
- **1918**: War ends with an armistice in November, leading to significant loss of life and societal changes.

### Post-War Era (1918-1939)
- **1921**: Establishment of the Irish Free State following the Anglo-Irish War, while Northern Ireland remains part of the UK.
- **1926**: The General Strike occurs, marking a pivotal moment in labor relations.
- **1929**: The Wall Street Crash triggers a severe economic downturn in Britain.

## World War II (1939-1945)
- **1939**: Britain declares war on Germany following its invasion of Poland.
- **1940**: Winston Churchill becomes Prime Minister; significant events include the Battle of Britain and extensive bombing raids on British cities.
- **1944**: D-Day invasion marks a turning point as Allied forces land in Normandy.
- **1945**: Germany surrenders in May, leading to celebrations but also challenges in post-war recovery.

## Post-War Period (1945-1979)

### Social Changes and Decolonization
- **1948**: Establishment of the National Health Service (NHS) providing healthcare to all citizens.
- **1947**: India gains independence, marking a significant moment in British decolonization efforts.
- **1956**: The Suez Crisis occurs, showcasing Britain's waning global influence.

### Political Developments
- **1979**: Margaret Thatcher becomes Prime Minister, initiating significant economic reforms and a shift towards free-market policies.

## Modern Era (1980-Present)

### 1980s and 1990s
- **1982**: The Falklands War sees British forces reclaim the islands from Argentina.
- **1990**: Thatcher resigns; John Major takes over as Prime Minister.
- **1993**: The Downing Street Declaration sets a framework for peace in Northern Ireland.

### 21st Century Developments
- **2000s**: Continued discussions on devolution lead to increased powers for Scotland, Wales, and Northern Ireland.
- **2016**: The Brexit referendum results in a decision for the UK to leave the European Union, significantly impacting British politics and society.

## Conclusion

The history of Britain from 1900 to the modern period is marked by profound changes, including two world wars, social reforms, decolonization, and evolving political landscapes. Each era has contributed to shaping contemporary British society and its role on the global stage.
