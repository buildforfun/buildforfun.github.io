## The Timeline of Earth

The timeline of Earth encompasses its formation, geological developments, and significant biological events. Below is a structured timeline highlighting the major milestones in Earth's history.

### Formation and Early History (4.6 - 3.8 billion years ago)
- **c. 4.6 billion years ago**: Earth forms from the solar nebula, a disk of dust and gas surrounding the young Sun, through a process of accretion from smaller particles and planetesimals.
- **c. 4.5 billion years ago**: A Mars-sized body, Theia, collides with the early Earth, resulting in debris that eventually coalesces to form the Moon.
- **c. 4.4 billion years ago**: The surface of the Earth begins to cool and solidify, forming a primordial crust; volcanic outgassing creates a primordial atmosphere.
- **c. 4.0 billion years ago**: The first stable continental crust begins to form as the planet cools further.

### Hadean Eon (4.6 - 4.0 billion years ago)
- **c. 4.5 - 4.0 billion years ago**: The Hadean Eon is characterized by intense volcanic activity and frequent impacts from other celestial bodies, leading to a molten surface.

### Archean Eon (4.0 - 2.5 billion years ago)
- **c. 3.8 billion years ago**: The oldest known rocks are formed; evidence of early life appears in the form of stromatolites.
- **c. 3.5 billion years ago**: The first simple microbial life forms, primarily prokaryotes such as bacteria and archaea.

### Proterozoic Eon (2.5 billion - 541 million years ago)
- **c. 2.5 billion years ago**: Oxygen begins to accumulate in the atmosphere due to photosynthetic cyanobacteria, leading to the Great Oxidation Event.
- **c. 1.8 billion years ago**: The first eukaryotic cells emerge, marking a significant evolutionary step.
- **c. 600 million years ago**: The first multicellular organisms appear; the Ediacaran biota represents some of the earliest complex life forms.

### Phanerozoic Eon (541 million years ago - Present)
#### Paleozoic Era (541 - 252 million years ago)
- **c. 541 million years ago**: The Cambrian Explosion occurs, resulting in a rapid diversification of life forms in the oceans.
- **c. 485 million years ago**: Ordovician Period; first fish appear.
- **c. 420 million years ago**: Silurian Period; land plants begin to colonize terrestrial environments.
- **c. 360 million years ago**: Devonian Period; first amphibians evolve from fish.
- **c. 299 million years ago**: Carboniferous Period; extensive forests develop, leading to significant coal deposits.
- **c. 252 million years ago**: Permian-Triassic extinction event occurs, wiping out approximately 90% of species on Earth.

#### Mesozoic Era (252 - 66 million years ago)
- **c. 252 million years ago**: Triassic Period; dinosaurs begin to appear.
- **c. 200 million years ago**: Jurassic Period; dinosaurs dominate terrestrial ecosystems.
- **c. 66 million years ago**: Cretaceous-Paleogene extinction event occurs, likely caused by an asteroid impact, leading to the extinction of most dinosaurs.

#### Cenozoic Era (66 million years ago - Present)
- **c. 65 million years ago**: Paleogene Period; mammals begin to diversify and dominate after the extinction of dinosaurs.
- **c. 2 million years ago**: Quaternary Period begins; significant climatic changes lead to ice ages.
- **10,000 years ago**: End of the last glacial period; human civilizations begin to develop.

### Recent Geological Events
- **Present Day**: Earth's climate continues to change due to anthropogenic influences, including global warming and habitat destruction.

This timeline encapsulates significant geological and biological milestones in Earth's history, illustrating its dynamic nature and the evolution of life over billions of years. Each era marks critical developments that have shaped not only the planet but also the life forms that inhabit it today.
