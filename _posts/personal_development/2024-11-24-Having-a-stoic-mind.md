# Having an Iron Clad mind

Life is really complex, people say life is simple but it really isn’t. Our mind is a complex beast which most do not understand. With our uncontrolled mind we try and understand the mind of others and help others before correcting ourselves. This often leads to errors in judgement and more often than not grief for everyone involved.

What is the solution? I’m not sure there is exists THE solution but there may be A solution. One that I’ve found to be useful anyway. This solution is called Stoicism.  Stoicism allows you to take back control of your life in a very powerful way.

Why is stocism the solution? Stoicism teaches us that **your** **mind** is in control of how you **respond** to **external influences**. External influences include:

- Other people (family, friends and colleagues)
- Other World influences (war, climate change)
- The past (your mind reminding you of the past)
- Your body(thirst, hunger, cravings)

These external influences come to you and it is up to you how you respond. The only thing in your control is your response. How should you respond?

### Your response

Stocism teaches us that your response should be derived from your core principles and that the response should be as rational as possible. It teaches us not to allow the mind to be clouded by the emotional state, instead to rise above it and use rational thought to respond in the most effective way possible. A stoicsm aim is to maintain the tranquility inside their mind. Never forget: Your response is your responsibility. The four virtues of Stoicism are wisdom, courage, temperance, and justice.

- **Wisdom** – our ability to see things as they are, free from bias, and prejudice. Wisdom is our ability to see right from wrong, use reason, rational judgement, and avoid warping the truth of what we see.
- **Courage** – our ability to act in alignment with what we think is right, despite pressure not to.
- **Justice** – our capacity to behave in the interests of the common and wider good. The opposite of this would be to behave in ways that are detrimental to our neighbours and fellow man.
- **Temperance –** our ability to exercise self restraint, self control, moderation, and discipline

**Tranquility**

Tranquility is the art of ridding yourself of negative emotions. A tranquil person shows great self-control and won’t let her emotions dominate her intellect, for example by staying calm in a traffic jam, because she knows getting angry at traffic is useless.

## How to respond to negative people
People don’t like negative people. We try our best to remove the existance of negative people in our lives as much as possible, but somehow these people find a way in or we in situations where we cannot remove them. How should you handle negative people that insult you? Stoicism teaches us to be rational beings. Therefore, we must understand step by step what is happening.

### **Negative people (external influence)**

Negative people need attention and they want to control other people because they lack control of their own lives. If they can’t get what they want they will want to cause an explosion. They will cause an explosion using insults.

### The insult and how to react (your response)

The core meaning in stocism is that you control your response. When insults come there are multiple strategies we can use to respond. Do not in any circumstance face them head on as that’s what they want. Because if you engage with them head on that’s where they are strong and will inevitably lead to an explosion. Strategies for response are:

- Self acceptance – Accept that you are weird. If you aren’t ashamed of something, you won’t care if people tease you about it. Other people cannot make you feel shame, you make yourself feel shame. The shame comes from a misguided sense that you’re supposed to be perfect. You are not perfect. No one is perfect. Accept yourself fully.
- Think is what they have said true?
    - If yes, why be upset. They have stated the truth.
- Are they ignorant of something?
    - Inform them of the truth
- Do you even respect the person?
    - If yes, thank them for informing you.
    - If no, be relieved, you must be doing something right.
- Use humour. Humour can dispence tension in situations.
- Ignore and outwork them
- Give them positivity and a small amount of responsibility
