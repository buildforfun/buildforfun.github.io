## Learning Muay thai
Muay thai is a striking martial arts. It is often referred to as the art of 8 limbs becuase it uses both arms, elbows, knees, and feet to strike. I signed up to Muay thai in May 2022. I had put on quite a lot of weight during COVID and i wanted to shed it. Since then I have been going about once a week on average at least. I very recently signed up to twice a week. I love Muay Thai, for serveral reasons:
-  Few sports exercise as many parts of the body
-  The community aspect, everyone is really nice. Enhances the tribal feeling
-  The cardio benefits, each session burns about 1000 calories
-  It's self defence - i feel more physically confident
-  Post workout endorphins - after the workout + shower

#### My experience
After my first class, I couldn't move for ages. Body was aching and  
I struggled to remember long combinations. I was constantly thinking "oh I am going to look stupid I can't remember simple moves". However, the body is an amazing thing, it can adapt remarkably over time. Now I find it much easier to remember the moves. Eventually, I want to go to thailand to experience the muay thai culture.

#### Learning muay thai
The foundation of Muay Thai starts with proper stance and footwork. A strong stance provides balance and power, while effective footwork allows for fluid movement and positioning. 

Muay thai moves (my way of remembering moves):
- 1 = The Jab
- 2 = The Cross
- 3 = Hook
- 4 = Uppercut
- 5 = Body shot
- 6 = Elbow
- K1: Low kick
- K2 = Switch kick
- K3 = High kick
- K4 = Knee
- K5 = Teep

Some random tips:
----
  - Use punches first to distract the opponent. Then kick when they can't see it coming
  - Understand your range/bubble - will my hand/foot reach target?
  - Feint a lot - throws opponent off
  - See the opening for a kick - where are they exposed?
  - Whip hands faster to get more power on kicks
  - Practice switching often as feints to keep your opponent guessing.
  - Stay level during the switch to avoid telegraphing your intent.
  - Use the switch kick to set up other techniques, like a double switch followed by punches.
