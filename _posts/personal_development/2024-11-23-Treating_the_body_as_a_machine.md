# Treating the body as a machine
- Humans designed computers to operate efficiently and to do the work we didn't want to.  
- However, as we computers became more efficient, we became more lazy.
- To make sure we are operating as efficiently as possible. 
- It might be a good idea to learn from the machines we built.
- This document is me attempting to draw parallels between us and computers.

- The human entity can be split into 2 major components:
  -  Hardware is the body
  -  Mind is the software

## Hardware (the body)

When designing good hardware there are several traits to look for, such as:
- Good performance
- Good reliability
- Upgradability

These traits can be applied to humans too. The human body can become stronger if trained by exercising, it can become more reliable through practice and can be upgraded through good food and exercise. The main three axioms for hardware are:

1. **Exercise often:** this has benefits throughout software too.
2. **Healthy food only:** remember that the body can fast for a long long time.
3. **Sleep well:** this cleans the mind and body on a daily basis.

## Software (the mind)
The human mind is like the software running on the human hardware.

### Software input:
- Your sensory organs consume the input data: sound and sight being the primary ones.
- The information you feed your brain has to be off high quality.
- Consume content which will improve your thinking + develop you in your area of interest.
- All other junk content should be filtered. It's spam.

### Software instruction set:
- Grasp a foundational basics across multiple fields. Good computers have the ability to digest large amounts of information.
- **While you can't know everything you, you should convince yourself you can learn anything.**
- As you learn widely, you will stumble across fields that pique your interest. Learn those topics deeply from the basics up.
- Endeavour to build (in code) the topic from scratch. This beats reading and thinking you understand something, when in reality you probably don't.
- Build a lot of good quality projects, that can be shared and help the world.
- There will be obstacles, remember if it's out of your control, ignore it.
- Have the ability to shift from first person to third person.
- Try and get yourself to spend as much of your day in flow as possible. Move from task to task smoothly.
- Remember the future and your past is overrated. Stay in the present as much as possible and keep building.
